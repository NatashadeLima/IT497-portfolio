# Reflection

## Reflecting on My Spiritual and Professional Growth

This week, I took time to quietly reflect on my goals, my relationship with Heavenly Father, and the direction I want my life to take. During this time, I thought about the blessings I have received through my education, my family, and the opportunities I have had to learn and grow. I realized that my journey is about more than earning a degree or building a successful career. It is also about becoming the kind of person Heavenly Father wants me to be.

As I reflected, I felt grateful for the opportunity to study Information Technology at Ensign College. Every course, project, and challenge has helped me become more confident and prepared for my future career. At the same time, I have learned that technical knowledge alone is not enough. Qualities such as honesty, integrity, patience, and kindness are just as important as technical skills. I want my future work to reflect those values in the way I solve problems, work with others, and make ethical decisions.

One idea that stood out to me is that my spiritual and professional lives are connected rather than separate. My faith influences how I approach challenges, how I treat people, and how I use the knowledge I gain. Whether I am working on a technical project, helping a client, or protecting sensitive information, I want my decisions to reflect the values I believe in. My goal is to become an IT professional who is not only technically capable but also trustworthy, dependable, and willing to serve others.

This reflection reminded me that growth happens little by little. As I continue learning, I hope to strengthen both my testimony and my professional abilities. I believe that as I trust the Lord, continue studying, and do my best each day, He will guide me toward opportunities where I can use my skills to help others and make a positive difference.

---

## What I Learned

- My spiritual growth and professional growth support each other.
- Integrity is just as important as technical knowledge.
- Every learning experience helps prepare me for future opportunities.
- I want to use my education to serve others and make ethical decisions throughout my career.
